Positioning

Datasite built its brand on sell-side mandates. Investment banks love it. Corporate development teams pay for it and work around it. DealRoom was built for the buy side — pipeline, diligence, and integration in one place, at a price that doesn't scale with your deal volume.

We Win When...
  • Repeat acquirer doing 3+ deals/year
  • Team is lean, needs to scale without headcount
  • Using Excel + Dropbox + email today
  • Paying per-page or per-seat today
  • Need pipeline → diligence → PMI in one tool
  • Onboarding external advisors & counsel
Hard to Win When...
  • Deal mandated by sell-side bank using Datasite
  • Heavily regulated cross-border transactions
  • Legal team already locked in, switching cost high
  • Procurement is running a formal RFP requiring Fortune 1000 references

Objection Handling
Hear "We already use Datasite."
Say Makes sense — Datasite is the standard for the data room piece. My question is, what are you using for pipeline management and post-merger integration? Most teams we talk to are running Datasite for the data room and stitching together three other tools around it. We replace all of that.
Hear "Datasite is the industry standard."
Say It is — for sell-side mandates where the bank is running the process. You're on the buy side. The tools the banks use for managing auctions aren't built for how corp dev teams run acquisitions. Datasite wasn't designed for pipeline, diligence requests, or PMI. DealRoom was.
Hear "Datasite has better security / compliance."
Say Both platforms meet enterprise security standards — 256-bit encryption, role-based permissions, full audit trail. If you're running cross-border deals with multi-jurisdiction regulatory requirements, Datasite may have an edge there. But if you're doing domestic acquisitions, the security posture is equivalent and DealRoom gives you the full workflow Datasite can't.
Hear "Datasite is what our legal team knows."
Say That's usually the biggest switching cost — and it's real. Two things worth considering: first, DealRoom is rated easier to learn than Datasite on G2, so ramp is fast. Second, every external party you add to Datasite costs you a seat. With DealRoom, your counsel, advisors, and sellers are all unlimited. That adds up quickly.
Hear "We don't know what Datasite costs but we're used to it."
Say That's actually the issue — Datasite doesn't publish pricing because it's per-page, per-user, and per-MB of storage. Most teams only find out what they're paying when the invoice comes in after a deal closes. DealRoom is flat-rate. You know exactly what you're paying regardless of deal size or team size.
Hear "Send me something to review."
Say Happy to — but honestly, DealRoom is a platform that shows better than it reads. Rather than a PDF, can we do a 20-minute screen share? I'll show you exactly how a deal flows from pipeline to close. If it's not a fit, I'll tell you.
Discovery Questions
1
How many deals are you running concurrently right now — and where does each one live?
2
When a deal closes, how does your team hand off from diligence to integration? What gets lost in that transition?
3
How do you manage diligence requests today — email threads, shared drives, something else?
4
When you add outside counsel or advisors to a deal, how are you handling access and document sharing?
5
How long does it take to spin up a new deal workspace today? And how much of that is manual setup?
6
When you report deal status to the CFO or board, where does that data come from?
Landmine Questions Ask these to expose Datasite gaps
"What does your team use for pipeline tracking — is that inside Datasite or somewhere else?"
Datasite has no pipeline module. Forces them to admit they're using another tool.
"After a deal closes, do you stay in Datasite for integration or move everything to a different system?"
Datasite has no PMI tools. Exposes the workflow gap immediately.
"When you bring in outside counsel or advisors, how do you handle additional user seats in your current tool?"
Surfaces Datasite's per-seat cost pain. Lets the prospect do the math.
"Do you know what you paid Datasite on your last deal once storage and page overages were included?"
Most don't. Creates pricing anxiety and opens the conversation to DealRoom's flat-rate model.
Never Do This
Don't say Datasite is bad. Say it's built for a different use case. You never look threatened.
Don't lead with price. Lead with workflow gaps. Price becomes the closer, not the opener.
Don't claim equal security for highly regulated cross-border deals. Acknowledge Datasite leads there — then reframe around their actual use case.
Don't send a PDF when they say "send me something." Push for a 20-minute demo. The product closes; the PDF gets filed.

  • Paying seat-based or per-page fees today
  • CFO involved and asking about deal ROI/efficiency

  • Cross-border deals requiring multi-jurisdiction compliance
